Bridging the gap between digital logic and analog realities. A high-speed, transparent alternative to proprietary industrial relays.

Explore the Project

Securing the Grid with Microsecond Precision

The stability and reliability of a power grid depend critically on the rapid detection and isolation of electrical faults[cite: 69]. While traditional protection systems have historically relied on relays and microcontroller-based designs, modern solutions are increasingly adopting FPGAs for their parallelism, low-latency response, and re-configurability[cite: 70].

High-Speed Response

Highlighting the deterministic, microsecond response time of hardware-level parallel processing over traditional sequential software[cite: 83].

Hardware Integration

Building real-time, mixed-signal hardware solutions utilizing Verilog RTL design on the Artix-7 (Basys-3) board with robust 10-bit ADC interfacing.

Cost & Accessibility

Positioning the system as a highly customizable, transparent alternative to "black-box" proprietary industrial relays[cite: 84].

The Market Gap & Strategy
