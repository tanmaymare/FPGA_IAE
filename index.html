FPGA-Based Real-Time Fault Detection and Protection System

Bridging the gap between digital logic and analog realities. A high-speed, transparent alternative to proprietary industrial relays for Power Grid Applications.

Explore the Project Support Us

Securing the Grid with Microsecond Precision

The stability and reliability of a power grid depend critically on the rapid detection and isolation of electrical faults. While traditional protection systems have historically relied on relays and microcontroller-based designs, modern solutions are increasingly adopting FPGAs for their parallelism, low-latency response, and re-configurability[cite: 70].

High-Speed Response

Highlighting the deterministic, microsecond response time of hardware-level parallel processing over traditional sequential software.

Hardware Integration

Building real-time, mixed-signal hardware solutions utilizing Verilog RTL design on the Artix-7 (Basys-3) board with robust 10-bit ADC interfacing.

Cost & Accessibility

Positioning the system as a highly customizable, transparent alternative to "black-box" proprietary industrial relays.

Pitch Deck Overview

Strategic analysis mapping out our path from academic project to industrial application.

Problem & Solution

Problem: Lack of publicly available research due to the proprietary nature of FPGA solutions in industry settings.

Solution: We provide an open-source, highly customizable baseline design utilizing hardware-in-the-loop testing, breaking the barrier of closed-source industry standards.

Market & Adoption

Adoption: Targets academic research labs needing testing frameworks, Collabrating with colleges for beyond course content, expanding to factories needing rapid motor protection, and eventually smart grid developers.

Competition: Unlike proprietary "black-box" relays, our solution offers transparency, configurability, and lower entry costs.
